I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Institute for Global Food Security, School of Biological Sciences, Queen's University Belfast. My work sits at the intersection of toxicology, food safety, and human health, with a focus on the effects of endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs) on the human body.

I use in vitro models and high-content analysis to understand how environmental pollutants affect reproductive and metabolic health — and how dietary nutrients might help mitigate those effects. I am currently part of the EU FREIA project, developing bioassays to identify chemicals that may impair female reproductive function.

Research Interests

Endocrine-disrupting chemicals · in vitro toxicology · female reproductive health · obesity and diabetes pathways · persistent organic pollutants · dietary intervention · high-content analysis and reporter-gene assays. See Research for details.
