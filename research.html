Research Overview

My research investigates how endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs) and persistent organic pollutants affect human health, and how dietary nutrients can be used to mitigate their harmful effects. I combine in vitro cell models with imaging and molecular assays to study reproductive and metabolic endpoints.

Projects

FREIA

FREIA — Female Reproductive Toxicity of EDCs

As part of the EU Horizon 2020 FREIA project, I develop and optimise in vitro bioassays to identify chemicals that may impair female reproductive function, contributing improved test methods for regulatory toxicology.

PhD

EDCs, Obesity & Diabetes Pathways, and Dietary Intervention

My doctoral research examined the effects of endocrine-disrupting persistent organic pollutants on obesity and diabetes pathways using in vitro models of the gastrointestinal system and adipose tissue, and tested dietary nutrients as a strategy to mitigate the observed detrimental effects.
